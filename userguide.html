Getting the most out of PULSE

Everything you need to link your account, generate your first report, and use each part of the dashboard.

Get started The Teams agent Weekly Milestones Execution Tracker CAF nominations FY Connect How it works Safety & trust FAQ
What PULSE is: a Microsoft-internal command centre for Solution Engineers — a Teams / Copilot agent plus a web dashboard. It reads your MSX pipeline and Microsoft 365 activity and turns them into your weekly report, motion scorecard, CAF nominations, and FY Connect. You sign in with your Microsoft account and only ever see your own territory.

1 · Get started ONE-TIME SETUP

PULSE isn't in the company app store yet, so getting the agent is a quick one-time setup — about five minutes. After that, everything runs from Teams and the dashboard.

Before you start, you'll need:
  • An active MSX seat — PULSE only ever shows your own MSX (Dataverse) data, so you must be a licensed MSX user. If you use MSX today, you're all set; if you can sign in but see no data, this is almost always why.
  • A Microsoft 365 Copilot licence (the agent runs inside Copilot).
  • Custom app upload (sideloading) enabled for you in Teams — most Microsoft SEs already have this. If not, see “If neither option works” below.

Step 1 — Get PULSE added to your Teams

There are two ways. Option A is the quickest and needs no file.

Option A — Ask Daphne to add you as a collaborator RECOMMENDED · NO FILE

  1. Email Daphne your work address and ask to be added as a collaborator on the PULSE agent. (She adds you in the Teams Developer Portal — takes seconds.)
  2. Once added, go to dev.teams.microsoft.com → Apps and open PULSE.
  3. Click Preview in Teams. PULSE installs straight into your Teams / Copilot — no download or upload.

Option B — Upload the package yourself

  1. Ask Daphne for the package file PULSE-agent-v1.0.55.zip and save it (don't unzip it).
  2. In Microsoft Teams, open Apps (left rail, or the ··· menu) → Manage your apps.
  3. Click Upload an app → Upload a custom app, pick the .zip, and confirm Add.

Either way, PULSE now appears in Microsoft 365 Copilot in your agents list. Pin it so it's easy to find.

If neither option works — “Preview in Teams” or “Upload a custom app” is greyed out or missing — your tenant hasn't enabled custom app upload for you. Email Daphne; PULSE can instead be published to the org app catalogue by a Teams admin, after which you'll simply find “PULSE” in Apps with nothing to install.

Step 2 — Link your account (one time)

  1. Open the PULSE link page, sign in with your Microsoft account, and copy your one-time code — it looks like ABCD-EFGH.
  2. Open PULSE in Copilot and say “generate my report.” When it asks, paste your code. You only ever link once.

Step 3 — Generate & save

Say “save to dashboard.” Your dashboard now populates — Milestones, Tracker, CAF and Connect all light up with your data.

Until you've saved at least one report, the Tracker, Connect and Account pages stay empty on purpose — they'll unlock automatically once your first report is saved.

2 · The Teams agent

Once PULSE is added (section 1) and pinned, open it inside Microsoft 365 Copilot. The agent is where reports are generated. A few things you can say:

generate my report save to dashboard what's changed since last week?

The agent dates each piece of evidence by when the activity actually happened (from the content), not when it was synced — so a fresh run can still add older-dated items correctly.

3 · Weekly Milestones

Your headline view. Each MSX milestone becomes a card with an AI-drafted weekly progress narrative built from your real meetings, emails and follow-ups.

What you can do

  • Review & edit the drafted progress before it's final.
  • Classify evidence as Direct, Partner or Internal.
  • Push to CRM — send the update back as a forecast comment (no date-stamp prefix; PULSE files it against the right fiscal week).
  • Exclude a card from CRM push with the per-card toggle.
  • Switch fiscal year with the FY selector; prior FYs appear only if they have saved reports.

CRM write-back

When you push, PULSE appends a milestone or opportunity comment and creates any Hands-on-Keyboard (HoK) task activities — it never overwrites an existing comment or edits/closes an existing task, and it de-duplicates. Nothing is written to MSX until you approve it.

Other captured activity: work whose milestone is closed or cancelled isn't forced onto the wrong milestone. PULSE captures it separately, and you can post it as an opportunity-level forecast comment on an open opportunity instead — again, only on your confirmation.

4 · SE Execution Tracker

A scorecard across eight strategic SE motions, scoped to your discipline (via RAIN Qualifier 2, so a Data SE and an Apps SE see different primary motions).

  • Motion coverage — attach % and the ACR sitting in the gap for each motion.
  • Territory whitespace — unattached opportunities with timing / priority and a fit-scored AI attach play per account.
  • Cockpit brief — a short “where to spend today” summary.
The solution-area filter and the whitespace cards all follow the same label, so what you filter is what you see.

5 · CAF nominations

PULSE watches the Cloud Accelerate Factory data and flags opportunities that are eligible but not nominated, stalled, or declined.

Two ways to nominate

  • Nominate via Azure Offer Navigator — PULSE assembles the full packet (fields laid out in the same sections as the AON form, with the specialist and co-sell partner auto-resolved). Copy it in, review, and submit. PULSE never submits for you.
  • See example — preview a sample packet to see exactly what you'll get before a real opportunity qualifies.

6 · FY Connect

Capture evidence continuously through the year, then generate your Connect prose on demand.

  • Add evidence any time — wins, workshops, feedback. There's no fixed cadence.
  • Generate over any window you choose; PULSE classifies evidence to the goal framework (goals 1–5) and writes the prose.
  • Save snapshots to revisit and refine later.

7 · How PULSE works ARCHITECTURE

PULSE is hosted and per-user — there's nothing to install and every signed-in SE sees only their own pipeline. Here's the path a request takes:

🧑‍💻 YouTeams / M365 Copilot → 🤖 PULSE agentdrafts your report → 🛡️ Secure gatewayauth · rate-limit · audit → ⚙️ PULSE APIsAzure Functions → 🗄️ Your MSX & M365Dataverse · mail · chat · calendar → 📊 Your dashboardsMSAL sign-in
  • The agent is a Microsoft 365 declarative agent. It gathers your week and drafts the report, using Copilot's native Graph grounding to read your M365 activity (mail, chat, calendar).
  • The secure gateway fronts every agent call — it authenticates the request, applies rate limits, and writes a central audit trail. The APIs reject any call that hasn't come through it.
  • The PULSE APIs build and persist your report, hold your Connect evidence, compute territory whitespace, and stage CRM write-backs. Each caller is resolved through a single trusted-identity check (your sign-in, or a one-time link code).
  • Your data lives in MSX (Dataverse). Reports and Connect evidence are stored per-user, keyed to your account id — so your data is isolated from every other SE's.
  • The dashboards are a secure web app. You sign in with your Microsoft account and read only your own per-user data.

8 · Safety, privacy & trust

PULSE is deliberately conservative about anything that touches MSX or your data:

  • The agent suggests; the dashboard writes. Every HoK activity, CRM comment and CAF nomination is surfaced for you to confirm — the agent never writes on its own.
  • Append-only / create-only. Existing comments are never overwritten, existing tasks are never modified or closed, and duplicates are detected.
  • Evidence is classified by who attended. A call with only a partner present (no customer) is Partner, never Direct — even when it concerns that customer. Partner and Internal evidence never auto-push to CRM.
  • Per-user isolation. Every report and Connect store is keyed to your account id; you only ever see your own data.
  • HoK is status-truth. Hands-on-Keyboard reflects your MSX records, never an inferred value.
  • Active-contribution rule. Only evidence where you actively contributed is included.

FAQ

Do I need to install anything?

No desktop app — but because PULSE isn't in the company app store yet, you add it once through Microsoft 365 Copilot. The quickest way is to ask Daphne to add you as a collaborator, then click Preview in Teams from dev.teams.microsoft.com → Apps — no file needed. Full steps in Get started.

How do I actually get the agent?

Option A (recommended): ask Daphne to add you as a collaborator, then open dev.teams.microsoft.com → Apps → PULSE → Preview in Teams. Option B: ask her for PULSE-agent-v1.0.55.zip and upload it via Teams → Apps → Manage your apps → Upload a custom app. See Get started.

“Preview in Teams” / the upload option is greyed out — what now?

Your tenant hasn't enabled custom app upload (sideloading) for you — this affects both options. Email Daphne; the agent can be published to the org app catalogue by a Teams admin instead, so you'll just find “PULSE” in Apps with nothing to install.

Can other SEs see my data?

No. You sign in with your Microsoft account and PULSE only ever shows your own territory. Pages stay empty until you save a report.

Will it edit MSX without me?

No. PULSE drafts and assembles; you review and approve every push, comment and nomination. Comments are only ever appended and tasks are only ever created — nothing existing is overwritten or closed.

Where is my data stored?

In MSX (Dataverse), stored per-user against your own account id — so it's isolated from other SEs. Your M365 activity is read live through Microsoft Copilot's Graph grounding when a report is generated; PULSE doesn't keep a separate copy of your mail or chat.

Why is a page empty?

You haven't saved a report yet. Generate one in the Teams agent and say “save to dashboard.”
